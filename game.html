📋 The 6 Magic Numbers
Learn these rules — you'll use them when filling in the Detective Table!
✓ CHECK = GOOD (1 point)
~ TILDE = OKAY (0 points)
✗ CROSS = WARNING (0 points)
1
Stock Price ($)
How much ONE share costs right now. Compare it to the 52-week range to know if it's cheap or expensive.
🏷️ Like a sticker price — but is it on sale?
  • ✓Any price is OK — compare to 52-Wk range
  • ~Rising very fast lately? Be careful
  • ✗Near all-time high + bad P/E = skip it
📍 Where to find it: It's the big number at the very top of the page on both Yahoo Finance and Google Finance!
2
P/E Ratio
Imagine you buy a lemonade stand for $200. It earns $10/yr profit. You'd wait 20 years — so P/E = 20. Lower = better deal!
🍋 Stand costs $100, earns $10/yr → P/E 10 → great deal!
  • ✓Under 25 = money back in less than 25 years
  • ~25–50 = pricey but growing fast
  • ✗Over 50 = very expensive!
📍 Yahoo Finance: look for "PE Ratio (TTM)" on the Summary page.
Google Finance: look for "P/E ratio" in the right panel.
(TTM = Trailing Twelve Months = last 12 months of profits)
3
52-Week High / Low
The highest and lowest price in the past year. Shows if the stock is "on sale" or overpriced compared to recent history.
🏷️ Like knowing the original vs. sale price.
  • ✓Near 52-wk LOW = possibly on sale!
  • ~In the middle = normal
  • ✗Near 52-wk HIGH = might be too expensive
📍 Yahoo Finance: look for "52 Week Range" on the Summary page — it shows the Low and High in one line (e.g. 57.00 – 74.00).
Google Finance: look for "52-wk high" and "52-wk low" listed separately in the right panel.
4
Market Cap
Total value of ALL shares. Shows how big the company is. Bigger usually means safer!
🏙️ Small shop vs. big mall vs. giant city!
  • ✓Large Cap (>$10B) = safer, stable
  • ~Mid Cap ($2B–$10B) = some risk
  • ✗Small Cap (<$2B) = riskier
📍 Yahoo Finance & Google Finance: both label it "Market Cap".
They shorten big numbers — enter it in billions: T = Trillion (2.8T → enter 2800), B = Billion (280B → enter 280).
5
Revenue Growth %
Is the company making MORE money than last year? Positive = growing business. Negative = shrinking!
🌱 Is it growing taller or wilting?
  • ✓Over +10% = fast growing!
  • ~0% to +10% = steady
  • ✗Negative = shrinking!
📍 ⚠️ Trickiest one to find!
Yahoo Finance: click the "Statistics" tab → scroll down to the "Income Statement" section → look for "Revenue Growth (YoY)".
Google Finance: click "Financials" → look at the Revenue bar chart and compare this year vs last year.
6
Dividend Yield %
Money the company pays you JUST for holding the stock — like pocket money from your investment!
🐔 Like a hen that lays eggs — the eggs are your dividend!
  • ✓Over 2% = bonus income!
  • ~Under 2% but >0% = a little
  • ✗0% = no dividend (OK if growing fast)
📍 Yahoo Finance: look for "Forward Dividend & Yield" on the Summary page — use the % number in brackets (e.g. 3.12%).
Google Finance: look for "Dividend yield" in the right panel.
If it shows N/A or —, the company pays no dividend → enter 0.

📊 finance.yahoo.com ↗ 🔍 google.com/finance ↗
