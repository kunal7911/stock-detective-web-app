📋 The 6 Magic Numbers — Cheat Sheet
Use these rules when you fill in the Detective Table. Each number tells you something different about a company!
✓ CHECK = GOOD (1 point)
~ TILDE = OKAY (0 points)
✗ CROSS = WARNING (0 points)
1
Stock Price ($)
How much ONE share costs right now. Like a price tag at a store. The price alone doesn't say if it's cheap or expensive — you need to compare it to the 52-week range!
🏷️ Like the sticker price of a toy at a shop.
  • ✓Any price is OK — compare to 52-Wk range
  • ~Rising very fast lately? Be careful
  • ✗Near all-time high + bad P/E = skip it
2
P/E Ratio
Imagine buying a lemonade stand for $200 that earns $10/year profit. You'd wait 20 years to get your money back — so P/E = 20. Lower P/E = better deal!
🍋 Stand costs $500, earns $10/yr → P/E = 50 → wait 50 years! Too expensive!
  • ✓Under 25 = Get money back in less than 25 years. Good!
  • ~25 to 50 = Pricey but growing fast = maybe OK
  • ✗Over 50 = Very expensive! Would wait 50+ years!
3
52-Week High / Low
The highest and lowest price in the past year. Shows if the stock is "on sale" right now or overpriced compared to its recent history.
🏷️ Like knowing an item's original price vs. today's sale price.
  • ✓Near 52-wk LOW = possibly on sale! Great deal!
  • ~In the middle = Normal price range
  • ✗Near 52-wk HIGH = might be too expensive right now
4
Market Cap
Total value of ALL shares added together. Shows how big the company is. Bigger usually means safer, like a giant mall vs. a tiny shop!
🏙️ Small shop vs. big mall vs. giant city!
  • ✓Large Cap (>$10B) = Safer, more stable
  • ~Mid Cap ($2B–$10B) = Some risk, some reward
  • ✗Small Cap (<$2B) = Riskier — be careful!
5
Revenue Growth %
Is the company making MORE money than last year? A positive number means the business is growing. Negative means it's shrinking!
🌱 Like a plant — is it growing taller or wilting?
  • ✓Over +10% = Fast growing! Exciting!
  • ~0% to +10% = Steady and stable
  • ✗Negative = Shrinking! Big warning sign!
6
Dividend Yield %
Money the company pays you JUST for holding the stock. Like pocket money from your investment — even if the price doesn't change, you get paid!
🐔 Like a hen that lays eggs — the eggs are your dividend!
  • ✓Over 2% = Bonus income! Great!
  • ~Under 2% but >0% = A little pocket money
  • ✗0% = No dividend — OK if growing very fast
🌐 Where to find these numbers? Search the company ticker (e.g. AAPL, TSLA) on finance.yahoo.com, google.com/finance, or marketwatch.com
